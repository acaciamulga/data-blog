understanding basis of record living specimen, preserved specimen, machine observation

John Waller
2019-09-06
GBIF

Recently a user noticed that there were Asian Red Pandas (Ailuridae) occurring in North America.

The vocabulary used by GBIF comes from TDWG. https://dwc.tdwg.org/terms/#livingspecimen

https://api.gbif.org/v1/image/unsafe/https%3A%2F%2Fapp.coeli.cat%2Fcoeli%2FICUB-NAT%2FHeritageObject%2FN909033%2FportraitMedia

This user correctly looked to basis of record to diagnose the problem.

But all of the records were “Preserved Specimen”.

Did a musuem staff member make a mistake?

Sometimes a preserved specimen will be harvested for a museum from a zoo. In such cases, the animal never occurred naturally. It went from “living specimen - > preserved specimen” so to speak, so the musuem staff correctly labeled the specimen.

https://www.gbif.org/occurrence/map?taxon_key=9673
